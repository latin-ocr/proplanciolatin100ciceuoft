of followers (salutatores, deductores and adsectatores), be lavish
of his presence in public (semper cum multitudine esse), ‘dive
into men's hearts with humble and familiar courtesy⁷3‚' canvass
all thoroughly and in good earnest, make liberal promises of pro-
fessional assistance (opera), refusing no application, and must not
take a definite part in politics but manage so as to please all parties.
Cicero refers to all these means of permitted ambitus in the
present speech⁷4‚ he employed them himself at the time of his
own candidature and at a later period as the supporter (fautor)
of his younger friends.
